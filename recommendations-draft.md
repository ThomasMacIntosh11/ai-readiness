# Final Report: Proposed Recommendations Copy

User flow: 3 recommendations under "What to focus on in the next 90 days."

- **Rec #1**: based on the user's overall maturity stage (5 options, 1 per stage)
- **Recs #2 & #3**: based on the user's two lowest-scoring domains (14 options total: 2 per domain, where under 50% gives the foundational version and 50% or higher gives the intermediate or advanced version)

Format: one header (the action) and a single sentence explanation underneath.

---

## Recommendation #1: Stage-based (5 options)

### Explorer (0 to 25%)
**Get interested people in a room to try one test case and capture what they learn.**
Bring together a small group of curious volunteers to pick one practical problem, try AI on it, and document the lessons so each attempt makes the organization smarter for the next one.

### Pilot (26 to 50%)
**Pick 3 to 5 use cases and build them with a small side-of-desk team.**
Standing up a lean cross-functional team to ship rough versions of a handful of use cases gives you the hands-on experience and pattern recognition you need before investing at scale.

### Builder (51 to 70%)
**Take one proven pilot enterprise-wide and document what worked and what didn't.**
Turning your most successful pilot into a true enterprise rollout builds the muscle for repeatable scale-ups and ensures the next one is faster, cleaner, and lower risk.

### Integrator (71 to 85%)
**Formalize AI governance across leadership and hunt for cross-enterprise value plays.**
A formal executive governance forum with real decision rights lets you move beyond isolated wins and capture value that no single function could deliver alone.

### Transformer (86 to 100%)
**Redesign your operating model for a hybrid human and agentic workforce.**
Rebuilding org structure, performance management, incentives, and decision rights for a world where humans oversee agents is what unlocks the next leap in productivity and growth.

## Recommendations #2 and #3: Domain-based (14 options, 2 per domain)

### 1. Strategy and ambition

**Under 50%: Get clear on why AI matters for your business.**
Agreeing on a simple, shared "why" gives every team a single anchor for decisions so AI effort stops fragmenting across functions.

**50% or higher: Map how AI directly supports your corporate strategy.**
Connecting AI explicitly to your strategic priorities ensures investment goes where it moves the business, not just where it's easiest to deploy.

### 2. Data and analytics foundation

**Under 50%: Identify the 5 to 10 datasets behind your top use cases and fix quality, access, and ownership for those first.**
Concentrating data effort where AI is actually being used unblocks priority initiatives much faster than trying to fix everything at once.

**50% or higher: Build reusable data products with documented lineage and APIs that AI can consume directly.**
This stops every new use case from reinventing the data plumbing and lets AI act on live information rather than yesterday's snapshot.

### 3. Technology and AI infrastructure

**Under 50%: Pick a small, standard AI tool stack and put baseline security and cost controls around it.**
Standardizing early avoids paying twice for fragmented tooling and gives you visibility before AI spend and shadow IT get out of hand.

**50% or higher: Invest in orchestration, observability, and per-use-case cost visibility for agentic workloads.**
This lets you safely connect AI to production systems and scale agentic use cases without rearchitecting for each new model or tool.

### 4. Talent and digital literacy

**Under 50%: Roll out role-based AI training tied to the actual work people do, paired with tool access.**
Role-specific training applied to real work is what turns curiosity into daily productive use across the workforce.

**50% or higher: Have your AI-leading employees teach their peers directly.**
Peer-to-peer teaching from people doing the work spreads practical capability faster than any centralized training program could.

### 5. Operating model and agility

**Under 50%: Pick one important workflow and rebuild it from scratch with AI at the core.**
Redesigning instead of retrofitting shows the organization what AI-native work actually looks like and creates a template other teams can follow.

**50% or higher: Build lightweight scaling mechanisms (playbooks, scaling squads, internal showcases) to move pilots across business units.**
These mechanisms turn one-off wins into repeatable rollouts so successful AI moves quickly from a single team into shared enterprise value.

### 6. Governance and responsible AI

**Under 50%: Publish a one-page AI use policy covering data, accountability, and acceptable use.**
A clear baseline keeps employees moving confidently with AI and prevents the costly inconsistencies that appear when every team writes its own rules.

**50% or higher: Stand up an AI governance council with real authority to review outputs, track corrections, and prepare for regulation.**
Mature governance becomes a speed advantage by letting you ship AI faster because trust, compliance, and risk are managed by design.

### 7. Culture and change management

**Under 50%: Have leaders openly share AI experiments, including failures, in town halls and team forums.**
This signals that trying AI is safe and expected, which is the single biggest unlock for moving from cautious early users to broad adoption.

**50% or higher: Tie AI adoption and outcomes directly into leadership performance reviews and incentives.**
What gets measured gets done, so embedding AI into how leaders are evaluated is what makes culture change actually stick.

## Implementation notes

- **Lowest-score selection:** if two domains tie for lowest, suggest using a stable tie-breaker (for example, enabler order) so users with identical scores get a consistent report.
- **Edge case, all domains 50% or higher:** the rule still works (lowest of the high scores gets the intermediate rec) but consider adding a small note like "you're scoring well across all domains" so the framing matches reality.
- **Edge case, all domains under 50%:** you may want to flag that there are deeper foundational issues than 90 days can solve, even though the recs themselves are still actionable.
